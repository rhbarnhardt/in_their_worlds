RESOURCES

organizations

National Alliance on Mental Illness

www.nami.org   

NAMI is the national leader in educating and advocating for the complex issues surrounding mental illness. The organizations' goal is to create support networks for those dealing with mental illness and their families, as well as training for health care providers and government organizations. NAMI is a great resource for anyone looking to connect with other people dealing with mental illness, as well as crisis managment resources and finding mental health professionals

MentalHealth.gov from the Department of Health and Human Services

www.mentalhealth.gov   

This site features stories from patients, resources for the diagnosed and families, and helpful articles for navigating daily life with mental illness. This is also a great source for finding mental health professionals in your area, as well as national mental health hotlines.

Active Minds: College Campus Advocacy

www.activeminds.org   

Active Minds focuses on mental health issues in colleges and universities across the nation. This site can help you to find local chapters, learn how to be a mental health advocate, and find access to crisis managemnt resources.

ABOUT

In Their Worlds’ mission is to share the stories of those affected by mental illness, told in their own words, in order to create a more caring, informed, and supportive world.

We believe that stories connect us, and an honest voice can change long held beliefs. These stories educate those unfamiliar with the realities of mental illness, provide hope for the diagnosed and their families, and remind us of the strength that a supportive community can provide for an afflicted individual, whatever the specifics of that affliction may be.

In Their Worlds is hosted by Hunter Barnhardt, and is produced as his master’s thesis in interactive media for Elon University.

Special thanks to Jack Glenn and everyone from NAMI Guilford for their advice and guidance, Carolyn Crenshaw for her unparalleled production assistance, and Schay Barnhardt for her editorial voice and unending patience. I would also like to thank everyone who offered their stories. Your words will provide hope to others in ways we will never fully know.
